PHISHING EMAIL SAMPLE ANALYSIS – SAMPLE 1

Subject:
PayPal Account Verification Required

Sender Email:
support@paypa1.com

Greeting:
Dear Customer,


EMAIL MESSAGE
Your PayPal account has been temporarily limited due
to suspicious activity detected in your account.

To restore full access, please verify your account
immediately using the secure link below.

Verification Link:
http://paypal-security-login.com

Failure to complete verification within 24 hours
may result in permanent account limitation.

Thank you,
PayPal Security Team


PHISHING INDICATORS IDENTIFIED

1. Fake Sender Domain
The sender email address "support@paypa1.com" contains
a spelling manipulation where the letter "l" in PayPal
is replaced with the number "1". This technique is used
by attackers to imitate legitimate domains.

2. Generic Greeting
The email uses "Dear Customer" instead of the user's
real name. Legitimate services usually address users
personally.

3. Urgent Language
The email pressures the recipient to act quickly by
threatening account limitation within 24 hours.

4. Suspicious Link
The URL "http://paypal-security-login.com" is not an
official PayPal domain and may redirect users to a
fake login page designed to steal credentials.


EMAIL CLASSIFICATION
Type: Phishing Email
Risk Level: HIGH


ANALYSIS SUMMARY

This email attempts to impersonate PayPal and trick
users into clicking a malicious verification link.
If the user enters login credentials on the fake
website, attackers can capture the username and
password and gain unauthorized access to the
victim's PayPal account.

Users should always verify the sender domain and
avoid clicking links in suspicious or unexpected
emails.
